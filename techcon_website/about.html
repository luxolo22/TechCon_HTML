History of the TechCon

Tech conferences have evolved significantly over the years, driven by advancements in technology,
changes in industry needs, and the growth of the tech sector itself. Hereâ€™s a brief overview of their history:

  1. Early Beginnings: Tech conferences trace their origins to the mid-20th century when early computer scientists and engineers began to gather informally to discuss emerging technologies and share research.
    These gatherings were often small and focused on technical discussions rather than large-scale events.
  2. Emergence of Major Conferences: In the 1970s and 1980s, as computing technology became more mainstream and industries began to adopt it on a larger scale,
    conferences such as the ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) conferences and others started gaining prominence.
    These events were crucial for researchers, engineers, and academics to exchange ideas and showcase innovations.
